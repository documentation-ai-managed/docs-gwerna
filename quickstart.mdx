## Prerequisites

<Callout kind="info">
Before starting, ensure you have:
- A modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, or Safari)
- An email address for account verification
- Optional: Existing documentation in Markdown or GitHub repo for import
</Callout>

## Sign up for an account

Follow these steps to create your Gwerna account.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Visit signup page" icon="globe">
    Navigate to [https://gwerna.com/signup](https://gwerna.com/signup).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter details" icon="user">
    Provide your email, name, and a strong password. Gwerna uses secure hashing for passwords.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Verify email" icon="mail">
    Check your inbox for the verification link and click it to activate your account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Log in" icon="log-in">
    Return to [https://gwerna.com/login](https://gwerna.com/login) and sign in.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Create your first documentation space

A documentation space organizes your project's docs.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Access dashboard" icon="layout-dashboard">
    After login, you land at [https://dashboard.gwerna.com](https://dashboard.gwerna.com).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create space" icon="plus">
    Click **New Space**, enter a name like `My Project Docs`, and select a slug (e.g., `my-project`).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure basics" icon="settings">
    Choose public or private visibility and add a description.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and enter" icon="check-circle">
    Click **Create**. Your space is ready at `https://gwerna.com/my-project`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Import existing documentation

Gwerna supports seamless imports.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="GitHub Repo" icon="github">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Connect GitHub">
        In your space settings, select **Import from GitHub** and authorize the app.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Select repo">
        Choose your repo (e.g., `username/my-docs`) and branch (`main`).
      </Step>
      <Step title="Map files">
        Gwerna auto-detects Markdown files and imports them as pages.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Markdown Folder" icon="folder">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Upload ZIP">
        Prepare a ZIP of your Markdown files and upload via **Import ZIP**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Review structure">
        Gwerna preserves folder hierarchy as nested pages.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Edit and publish your first page

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create page" icon="file-plus">
    In your space, click **New Page** and name it `Introduction`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add content" icon="edit">
    Use the Markdown editor. Here's a sample:

    <CodeGroup tabs="Markdown,Preview">
    ````markdown
    # Welcome to My Project

    This is your **first page** in Gwerna.

    ## Features
    - Easy editing
    - Live previews
    - Custom domains
    ````

    ````html
    <h1>Welcome to My Project</h1>
    <p>This is your <strong>first page</strong> in Gwerna.</p>
    <h2>Features</h2>
    <ul>
      <li>Easy editing</li>
      <li>Live previews</li>
      <li>Custom domains</li>
    </ul>
    ````
    </CodeGroup>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish" icon="upload-cloud">
    Click **Publish**. Your page is live instantly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

</Callout>
